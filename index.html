Bridging gaps and extending reach in mental health research and practice

About me

Hi! I am a PhD Candidate in Clinical Psychology at Drexel University. I obtained a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology at Rutgers University and a Master of Science in Psychology at Drexel University.

As a researcher and clinician with experience working across academic and industry settings, my overall goal is to make evidence-based mental health care more accessible for those who need it. A major focus of my work is on leveraging digital technology to study mental health conditions in daily life and to develop, test, and implement scalable mental health supports for specific populations and settings.

I deeply value collaboration and mentorship, and I welcome opportunities to work with others who have similar interests. Please reach out if you'd like to connect!

Overall goals of my work

  • Make mental health care more accessible
  • Improve translation of mental health research to practice
  • Meet the needs and priorities of the communities that my work aims to serve

Methods I prioritize

  • Technology-enabled interventions co-developed with end-users
  • Dissemination and implementation science
  • Cross-field and sector collaborations
  • Mixed methods research
